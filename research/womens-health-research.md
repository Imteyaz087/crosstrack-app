## 5. SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE

### Dr. Stacy Sims — Menstrual Cycle Training Framework

**Low Hormone Phase (Menstrual/Early Follicular, Days 1-7):**
- Hormones at lowest = body most resilient to stress
- DO: High-intensity, heavy lifting, PRs
- Recovery capacity is HIGH

**Mid-Follicular Phase (Days 8-13):**
- Estrogen rising = anabolic environment
- DO: Heavy resistance, power work, sprint intervals
- Muscle building potential peaks

**Ovulation (Day ~14):**
- Estrogen peak = highest energy BUT ACL risk elevated
- DO: Strength + power, with extra warm-up
- CAUTION: Joint laxity increased

**Luteal Phase (Days 15-28):**
- Progesterone rises = higher body temp, more fatigue, higher cortisol
- DO: Moderate intensity, endurance, steady-state
- Increase carbs 10-15%, magnesium supplementation
- Need 30-60 min extra sleep

**Critical Sims Distinction:** Performance ≠ Training. You CAN hit a PR in any phase. But TRAINING load should be periodized with the cycle.

### IOC 2023 Consensus on RED-S

- REDs affects 15-80% of elite athletes
- Often goes unrecognized by athletes AND coaches
- Menstrual dysfunction is a key early warning sign
- New REDs Clinical Assessment Tool v2 (IOC REDs CAT2) available
- >170 research publications since 2018
- Low carbohydrate availability increasingly recognized as factor

### ACL Injury Risk by Cycle Phase

- Ovulatory phase = highest ACL injury risk
- Peak estradiol → increased ligament laxity
- Relaxin peaks Days 21-24 → additional ACL vulnerability
- Extra warm-up protocol recommended around ovulation

### Iron Deficiency in Female Athletes

- Up to 60% of female athletes are iron deficient
- 57.7% of Division I female athletes had low ferritin
- Menstruation causes ~10mg iron loss per cycle
- Iron deficiency reduces endurance by 3-4%
- Supplementation (100mg/day elemental iron) improves endurance 2-20%

## 6. FLO'S PRIVACY DISASTER — What to Avoid

**The Scandal:**
- Flo shared intimate health data (sex drive, masturbation habits, contraception, mental wellbeing) with Facebook, Google, and other tech companies
- No restrictions on how third parties could use the data
- Continued until exposed in February 2019
- FTC settlement in 2021 — required affirmative consent + independent privacy review
- Class action: Google paid $48M, Flo paid $8M = $56M total settlement

**Lessons for TrackVolt:**
1. ALL cycle data must stay local (IndexedDB only)
2. Zero third-party analytics on cycle screens
3. No Facebook SDK, no Google Analytics touching cycle data
4. Explicit privacy badge on every cycle screen
5. User can delete all cycle data with one button
6. User can export their data (CSV) any time
7. This becomes a MARKETING advantage against Flo
