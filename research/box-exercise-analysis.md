# Box Exercise Photo Analysis Report

## Summary
- 107 photos analyzed from Box CrossFit Album
- Photos span from late 2023 to mid 2025
- Box uses PushPress gym management system
- WOD board format: Warm Up | Strength | WOD with athlete scores on chalkboard
- Box programs a well-rounded mix of Olympic lifting, powerlifting, gymnastics, and conditioning

## WOD Formats Observed
- AMRAP (most common)
- EMOM
- For Time
- Tabata
- Interval (3 min on / 2 min off)
- Team WODs
- Buy-In + AMRAP
- Chipper style
- "Cindy" referenced as benchmark

## Strength Programming Patterns
- Every 90s or Every 2min x 5-7 sets
- Build to heavy single / 1RM
- Percentage-based (65%-90%)
- Rep schemes like 5-5-5-3-3, 6-6-6-6, 8-8-8-6-6
- Pause variations commonly used

## Scoring Levels
Three tiers consistently used:
- Scale (lighter weights)
- RX (prescribed weights)
- Elite (heavier weights)

## Notable Observations
- The box consistently programs 3-part classes: Warm Up → Strength → WOD
- Time caps typically 12-20 minutes
- Round goals given for AMRAPs
- Heavy emphasis on Olympic lifting skill work
- Gymnastics days focus on quality (5 rounds for quality format)
- Good variety of mono-structural movements mixed into WODs
- Benchmark WODs like "Cindy" appear in EMOM format
